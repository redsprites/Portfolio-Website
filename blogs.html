When you are washing the dishes

"Thich Nhat Hanh once said, 'When you're washing the dishes, that action should be the center of your universe. Just like when you're sipping tea, make that moment the axis on which your world turns.' I often find myself tangled up in thoughts about the future â€” will I land a job? Will I secure a work visa after college? Or I get lost reminiscing about past regrets. My mind is always on a trip, either to an uncertain future or a long-gone past, leaving me anxious or downcast.

I stumbled upon this wisdom while listening to Tim Ferriss's podcast: 'Be there when you're washing the dishes because if you're already dreaming about enjoying some peaches later, you'll miss out on the present moment. And guess what? When you're finally eating those peaches, your mind will already be somewhere else. ' It's like a cycle of constant yearning, and before you know it, you've missed out on actually living.

So, the lesson here? Be present. Savor the now. Because, at the end of the day, this very moment is all we really have.
